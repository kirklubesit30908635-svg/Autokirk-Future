# AutoKirk Current Vocabulary

**Status:** Public-safe vocabulary guide  
**Purpose:** Keep homepage, activation, demo, sales, and customer-facing language aligned without exposing internal design.

---

## Primary Hook

> Important work should not be marked complete without proof.

Use this when a person needs to understand AutoKirk quickly.

---

## Adoption Line

> AutoKirk connects to the tools you already use and keeps important work visible until the right proof exists.

Use this to explain that AutoKirk does not replace existing tools.

---

## Outcome Line

> When proof is sufficient, work can resolve automatically. When proof is missing, it stays visible, escalates, or fails.

Use this to explain the closeout loop.

---

## Category Line

> Proof-gated closure infrastructure.

Use this as the category phrase, but do not make it carry the full first-touch explanation by itself.

---

## Terms To Use Carefully

These can be useful, but should not lead the first-touch message:

- proof-gated closure
- closure layer
- closeout
- final-state record
- workflow
- infrastructure

Use them after the user already understands the plain-language problem.

## Wedges Are Not The Category

AutoKirk may enter the market through specific pain points, but those are wedges, not the defining direction.

Wedges:

- AI trust
- revenue leak prevention
- service closeout
- audit evidence
- employee handoffs
- internal operations
- customer promises

Category:

> Important work should not be marked complete without proof.

---

## Homepage Vocabulary Stack

Use this order:

1. Important work should not be marked complete without proof.
2. AutoKirk connects to the tools you already use and keeps important work visible until the right proof exists.
3. When proof is sufficient, work can resolve automatically. When proof is missing, it stays visible, escalates, or fails.
4. Proof-gated closure infrastructure.

The pain comes first. The category comes after.

---

## Activation Vocabulary Stack

Use this order:

1. Activate your AutoKirk link.
2. Choose where important work starts.
3. Define what proof means.
4. Keep work visible until proof exists.
5. Resolve, escalate, or fail.

---

## AI Vocabulary

Use:

> AI can create, suggest, and act. AutoKirk helps determine whether the result is acceptable to count as complete based on the user's rules, required proof, and evidence of how it got there.

Avoid making AutoKirk sound like the AI model, AI runtime, or AI orchestration layer.

---

## Drift Rule

If language starts making AutoKirk sound like generic workflow software, CRM, project management, revenue software, field-service software, or an AI dashboard, stop and rewrite toward:

> Important work should not be marked complete without proof.
